Is Norway Expensive To Visit? Travel Costs & Budgets For This Year

By Chris The Traveller

June 17, 2024

1. Accommodation Costs

Norway is known for its high cost of living, and accommodation is no exception. Expect to pay a premium for hotels, especially in major cities like Oslo and Bergen. However, there are budget options available, including hostels and Airbnb.

2. Food and Drink Prices

Eating out in Norway can be expensive, with dining at restaurants often costing significantly more than in other European countries. To save money, consider shopping at grocery stores and cooking your own meals, or try street food and local markets.

3. Transportation Expenses

Getting around Norway can be costly, particularly if you plan to travel long distances. Public transport is efficient but not cheap. Renting a car is an option for exploring rural areas, though fuel prices are high. Consider booking transport in advance to find deals.

4. Activity and Attraction Fees

Many of Norway's natural attractions, such as fjords and hiking trails, are free to visit. However, entry fees to museums and cultural sites can add up. Look for discount passes or free admission days to save money on these experiences.

5. Budget Tips for Visiting Norway

To make your trip to Norway more affordable, plan ahead and budget carefully. Use public transportation when possible, stay in budget accommodations, and take advantage of free attractions. Consider traveling during the off-season to find lower prices on flights and lodging.
